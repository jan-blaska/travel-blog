<Section>
  <MainHeader>Oulu – The City in the North</MainHeader>
  <ImageNextToText imageSrc="/adventures/europe/finland/oulu-and-lapland/12443653_1852964834930241_692979862_o.jpg" imageAlt="Oulu Finland" reverse={true}>
    <span className="text-lg md:text-xl text-(--green) font-bold">Cycling City and Student Life</span>
    <br />
    The city is very quiet and has very good infrastructure, especially for cyclists. People cycle there year-round — even at minus 30 degrees Celsius. That is truly incredible.
    <br />
    I quickly got used to it and soon explored the city on my bicycle. There are many ways to enjoy your time in this city, but I was mainly interested in traveling and discovering.
  </ImageNextToText>

<Section>
  <MainHeader>Kemi, Rovaniemi and the Lapland Experience</MainHeader>
  <ImageNextToText imageSrc="/adventures/europe/finland/oulu-and-lapland/IMG_9690.JPG" imageAlt="Kemi SnowCastle Finland">
    <span className="text-lg md:text-xl text-(--green) font-bold">Ice Castles and Santa Claus</span>
    <br />
    There are several places with very interesting attractions, such as ice sculptures. I visited two ice castles, including the famous Kemi SnowCastle. These are truly impressive places.
    <br />
    Near Oulu lies the well-known city of Rovaniemi, where Santa Claus Village is located. This place is right on the Arctic Circle. There you can meet Santa Claus and send postcards "from Santa Claus".
    <br />
    Finland is overall very quiet. The people seem reserved but are very kind and helpful. Overall I enjoyed this time very much and learned a great deal about myself.
  </ImageNextToText>
